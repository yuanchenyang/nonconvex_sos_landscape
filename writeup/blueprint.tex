\section*{Status}
This blueprint is not complete yet. The abstract SOCP component, orthogonal
mixing of the four factor coordinates, and all currently identified
representative-level rank-$4$ algebraic branches are now formalized. The
remaining gap is the normalization/classification step that reduces an
arbitrary admissible rank-$4$ factor to those representatives, followed by the
final theorem assembly in \texttt{TernaryQuarticProof.lean}.

\medskip
\noindent
One additional obstruction is now explicit. Orthogonal mixing acts on the
\emph{coefficient-space} preimages of the four coordinates, not directly on the
affine-linear polynomial space. So even when
$W \cap \mathbb{R}[x_1,x_2]_{\le 1}$ contains the lines of $1$, $x_1$, or
$x_2$, orthogonal mixing alone need not place those exact polynomials among the
coordinates. This is why an affine variable-change transport layer is needed in
addition to factor-coordinate mixing.

\section*{Formalized Step}
Let $r(u,p) = \sigma(u) - p$. The following statement is already formalized in
\texttt{TernaryQuarticProof/Certificate.lean}.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Lemma.} Assume:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $u$ is an admissible rank-4 point;
\item $u$ is a SOCP for $p$ with respect to a positive-definite bilinear form
$B$;
\item $r(u,p)$ is orthogonal to $\operatorname{im}(A_{u_{\mathrm{img}}})$ for
some admissible factor $u_{\mathrm{img}}$;
\item there is a decomposition
\[
  p = q_{\mathrm{img}} + \sum_{w \in W} \sigma(w),
\]
where $q_{\mathrm{img}} \in \operatorname{im}(A_{u_{\mathrm{img}}})$ and every
$w \in W$ is an admissible kernel direction for $A_u$.
\end{enumerate}
Then $r(u,p) = 0$.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Proof.}
The image term pairs to zero by the first-order condition. Each kernel-square
term pairs nonnegatively with the residual by the second-order condition,
because $A_u(w)=0$ removes the positive $2\langle A_u(w), A_u(w)\rangle$ part
of the Hessian expression. Summing these inequalities gives
$\langle p, r(u,p)\rangle \ge 0$. On the other hand, the first-order condition
at $v=u$ gives $\langle \sigma(u), r(u,p)\rangle = 0$, hence
\[
  \langle r(u,p), r(u,p)\rangle
  = \langle \sigma(u)-p, r(u,p)\rangle
  = - \langle p, r(u,p)\rangle \le 0.
\]
Positive-definiteness implies $\langle r(u,p), r(u,p)\rangle \ge 0$, so the
objective is zero and therefore $r(u,p)=0$.

\section*{Additional Formalized Reductions}
Two special cases are now formalized.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Image case.} If $p \in \operatorname{im}(A_u)$ with an admissible
preimage, then $r(u,p)=0$ directly from the first-order condition and positive
definiteness.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Constant-relation case.} If there exists a nonzero constant vector
$c \in \mathbb{R}^4$ with $\sum_i c_i u_i = 0$, then $r(u,p)=0$ for every SOS
quartic $p$.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Proof sketch.}
For each quadratic summand $q^2$ in an SOS decomposition of $p$, define the
admissible direction $w_i = c_i q$. Then
\[
  A_u(w) = q \sum_i c_i u_i = 0,
  \qquad
  \sigma(w) = \Bigl(\sum_i c_i^2\Bigr) q^2.
\]
Since $\sum_i c_i^2 > 0$, the SOCP inequality in the direction $w$ implies
$\langle q^2, r(u,p)\rangle \ge 0$. Summing over the SOS decomposition yields
$\langle p, r(u,p)\rangle \ge 0$, and the abstract SOCP argument finishes as
before.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Orthogonal factor-coordinate invariance.} Let $M \in \mathbb{R}^{4
\times 4}$ satisfy $M^\top M = M M^\top = I$, and define
\[
  (\operatorname{mix}_M u)_i = \sum_j M_{ij} u_j.
\]
Then the following are now formalized in
\texttt{TernaryQuarticProof/FactorTransform.lean}:
\begin{enumerate}
\item admissibility is preserved by $\operatorname{mix}_M$;
\item $A_{\operatorname{mix}_M u}(v) = A_u(\operatorname{mix}_{M^\top} v)$;
\item $\sigma(\operatorname{mix}_M u) = \sigma(u)$ and hence
$r(p,\operatorname{mix}_M u)=r(p,u)$;
\item FOCP and SOCP are preserved under orthogonal mixing.
\end{enumerate}
Therefore the eventual factor-space case analysis may be carried out up to
orthogonal recombination of the four coordinates.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Formalized representative surjectivity.} The constant
$\dim(W \cap \mathbb{R}[x_1,x_2]_{\le 1}) = 1$ model
\[
  u_{\mathrm{const}} = (1, x_1^2, x_1x_2, x_2^2)
\]
is now fully formalized in
\texttt{TernaryQuarticProof/RepresentativeSurjective.lean}. The key lemma is
that every quartic monomial of degree at most $4$ lies in
$\operatorname{im}(A_{u_{\mathrm{const}}})$, hence every quartic polynomial does
as well by linearity. Therefore every SOCP at this representative has zero
residual by the already-formalized image case.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Formalized surjective mixed-affine representative.} The model
\[
  u_{\mathrm{ma},15} = (1, x_1, x_1^2, x_2^2)
\]
is now formalized as well in
\texttt{TernaryQuarticProof/RepresentativeMixedAffine.lean}. Its proof is the
same style as the other surjective branches: every quartic monomial is hit
directly, either through the constant coordinate for total degree at most $2$,
through $x_1^2$ for monomials with $x_1$-degree at least $2$, or through
$x_2^2$ for the remaining quartic monomials. So every SOCP at this
representative has zero residual by the abstract image certificate.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Formalized codimension-one image reduction.} The mixed-affine
rank-$14$ representative
\[
  u_{\mathrm{ma},14} = (1, x_1, x_1x_2, x_2^2)
\]
now has a full representative-level formalization in
\texttt{TernaryQuarticProof/RepresentativeMixedAffine.lean}: if a quartic
polynomial has zero $x_1^4$ coefficient, then it lies in
$\operatorname{im}(A_{u_{\mathrm{ma},14}})$. The proof is again
monomial-by-monomial. Every quartic monomial except $x_1^4$ is represented by
one of the four coordinates, and then support induction upgrades the monomial
statement to all quartics with vanishing $x_1^4$ coefficient. The same file now
also proves that every SOCP at this representative has zero residual. The extra
step uses the six-monomial quadratic normal form to show that the $x_1^4$
coefficient of a quadratic square is exactly the square of its $x_1^2$
coefficient, and then matches that coefficient with the explicit kernel square
\[
  \sigma(0,0,-c x_1x_2, c x_1^2) = c^2(x_1^2x_2^2 + x_1^4).
\]

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Formalized codimension-two image reduction.} The mixed-affine
rank-$13$ representative
\[
  u_{\mathrm{ma},13} = (1, x_1, x_1^2, x_1x_2)
\]
is now also fully formalized in the same file. The image theorem proves that a
quartic lies in $\operatorname{im}(A_{u_{\mathrm{ma},13}})$ whenever its
$x_2^3$ and $x_2^4$ coefficients vanish. The key coefficient identities for a
quadratic square are
\[
  [x_2^3](q^2) = 2\,[x_2](q)\,[x_2^2](q), \qquad
  [x_2^4](q^2) = [x_2^2](q)^2,
\]
and the explicit kernel line
\[
  w_{b,c} = (0,0,-x_2(b+c x_2),x_1(b+c x_2))
\]
matches both missing quotient classes simultaneously. Hence every SOCP at this
representative also has zero residual.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Formalized span-three branch.} The representative
\[
  u_{\mathrm{span3}} = (1, x_1, x_2, h),
\]
with arbitrary quadratic homogeneous $h$, is still formalized directly in
\texttt{TernaryQuarticProof/RepresentativeSpanThree.lean}. But the file now
proves a stronger abstract theorem: for any admissible rank-$4$ factor $u$, if
there exist coefficient vectors $c_0,c_1,c_2 \in \mathbb{R}^4$ with
\[
  \sum_i c_{0,i}u_i = 1,\qquad
  \sum_i c_{1,i}u_i = x_1,\qquad
  \sum_i c_{2,i}u_i = x_2,
\]
then every SOCP at $u$ has zero residual. Thus the whole
$\dim(W \cap \mathbb{R}[x_1,x_2]_{\le 1})=3$ branch is now solved without any
exact orthogonal normalization to a rigid representative.

Writing a quadratic factor as
\[
  q = q_{\le 1} + q_{\mathrm{hom}}, \qquad
  q_{\mathrm{hom}} = x_1 a + x_2 b,
\]
with $a,b$ linear, the proof first shows that every cubic lies in
$\operatorname{im}(A_u)$ from the span relations above. It then builds the raw
kernel direction
\[
  w_q^{\mathrm{raw}}
  =
  \operatorname{rel}(-c_0,q_{\mathrm{hom}})
  + \operatorname{rel}(c_1,a)
  + \operatorname{rel}(c_2,b),
\]
which satisfies $A_u(w_q^{\mathrm{raw}})=0$. Its quartic part is
\[
  \sigma(w_q^{\mathrm{raw}})_{\deg 4}
  =
  \Bigl(\sum_i c_{0,i}^2\Bigr) q_{\mathrm{hom}}^2,
\]
so after scaling by
\[
  \alpha = \Bigl(\sqrt{\sum_i c_{0,i}^2}\Bigr)^{-1}
\]
one gets a kernel vector $w_q=\alpha\, w_q^{\mathrm{raw}}$ for which
\[
  q^2 - \sigma(w_q)
\]
has total degree at most $3$. Summing these cubic image terms over an SOS
decomposition of $p$ and using the indexed-family certificate in
\texttt{Certificate.lean} yields $r(u,p)=0$ for every SOCP in this branch.

\section*{Normalization Target}
The main unresolved issue is no longer the representative algebra above. It is
the remaining normalization/classification step for the branches not already
covered by the abstract span-three theorem, namely the independent
constant-containing $\dim = 2$ configurations that should reduce, after affine
variable changes and orthogonal mixing of the four coordinates, to one of the
already-formalized low-affine or mixed-affine representatives.

\medskip
\noindent
The affine-normalization layer is now split across
\texttt{TernaryQuarticProof/AffineTransform.lean} and
\texttt{TernaryQuarticProof/AffineSocpTransform.lean}. The first file handles
the raw affine substitution $x \mapsto A x + b$: the induced algebra
homomorphism preserves the quadratic and quartic degree bounds and commutes
with the maps $A_u(\cdot)$, $\sigma$, and the residual after applying the
substitution coordinatewise. The second file upgrades the invertible case to an
actual transport statement: after moving the bilinear form by the inverse
equivalence, affine changes of variables preserve positive-definiteness,
admissibility, SOS quarticity, FOCP, SOCP, and the condition $r(u,p)=0$.

What still remains is the classification lemma producing the specific affine
and orthogonal normalization data for those remaining rank-$4$ factors. Once
that data exists, the representative theorems can be pulled back through these
two transport layers.

\medskip
\noindent
That pullback step is now also packaged explicitly in
\texttt{TernaryQuarticProof/RepresentativeTransport.lean}. The main theorem
there says: if, after applying an affine inverse on the polynomial side and an
orthogonal mixing on the factor side, the SOCP point becomes an already-solved
representative $u_{\mathrm{rep}}$, then the original point has zero residual.
So the remaining normalization problem is now sharply isolated to one kind of
statement:
\[
  \text{produce exact data with }
  \operatorname{mix}(M^\top,\; e^{-1}\!\cdot u) = u_{\mathrm{rep}}.
\]

\section*{Correction To The Previous Case Split}
The earlier five-case list is not complete. The constant-containing
$\dim(W \cap \mathbb{R}[x_1,x_2]_{\le 1}) = 2$ branch splits further.

\medskip
\noindent
Representative mixed-affine models recorded in the Julia script are:
\[
  (1,x_1,x_1^2,x_1x_2),\qquad
  (1,x_1,x_1x_2,x_2^2),\qquad
  (1,x_1,x_1^2,x_2^2).
\]
Their image ranks are respectively $13$, $14$, and $15$, with missing quotient
monomials
\[
  \{x_2^3,x_2^4\},\qquad \{x_1^4\},\qquad \varnothing.
\]
Moreover the first two mixed-affine representatives also admit explicit kernel
directions:
\[
  u=(1,x_1,x_1^2,x_1x_2),\qquad
  w_\ell=(0,0,-x_2\ell,x_1\ell),\qquad \ell=c+d x_2,
\]
for which
\[
  A_u(w_\ell)=0,\qquad
  \sigma(w_\ell)=(x_1^2+x_2^2)\ell^2 \equiv x_2^2\ell^2
  \equiv 2cd\,x_2^3 + d^2 x_2^4
  \pmod{\operatorname{im}(A_u)}.
\]
Hence this family generates the two missing quotient classes. For
\[
  u=(1,x_1,x_1x_2,x_2^2),
\]
the direction
\[
  w=(0,0,-x_1x_2,x_1^2)
\]
satisfies
\[
  A_u(w)=0,\qquad \sigma(w)=x_1^2x_2^2+x_1^4 \equiv x_1^4
  \pmod{\operatorname{im}(A_u)}.
\]
So the mixed-affine algebra itself now looks tractable; the old statement that
the proof was complete up to normalization was too optimistic because the
classification step still has to produce these representatives.

\medskip
\noindent
The current candidate normalization for the constant-containing
$\dim = 2$ branch is the following. After choosing coordinates so that
\[
  W \cap \mathbb{R}[x_1,x_2]_{\le 1} = \operatorname{span}(1,x_1),
\]
the remaining quadratic plane is a $2$-dimensional subspace of
\[
  \operatorname{span}(x_1^2,x_1x_2,x_2^2),
\]
hence the kernel of a nonzero binary quadratic
\[
  q = a x_1^2 + b x_1x_2 + c x_2^2
\]
in the dual $3$-space. Under shears and rescalings preserving the line
$\mathbb{R}x_1$, the quadratic $q$ should reduce to one of
\[
  x_2^2,\qquad x_1^2,\qquad x_1x_2,\qquad x_1^2 \pm x_2^2.
\]
The numerical sweep now recorded in
\texttt{julia/tq\_r4\_exploration\_2026\_04\_09.jl} matches this exactly:
\[
  x_2^2 \leadsto \operatorname{rank} 13,\qquad
  x_1^2 \leadsto \operatorname{rank} 14,
\]
while
\[
  x_1x_2,\qquad x_1^2 \pm x_2^2,\qquad x_1^2 + x_1x_2,
\]
and the tested generic examples all have image rank $15$, with numerical
errors again near $10^{-10}$ or smaller. So the only nonsurjective
mixed-affine representatives appear to be
\[
  (1,x_1,x_1^2,x_1x_2),\qquad (1,x_1,x_1x_2,x_2^2).
\]
The written reduction is therefore no longer mysterious: it should be the
binary-quadratic completion-of-squares argument above, implemented inside the
subgroup of affine changes preserving the line $\mathbb{R}x_1$.

\medskip
\noindent
The first algebraic half of this reduction is now formalized in
\texttt{TernaryQuarticProof/MixedAffineNormalization.lean}. That file defines
the shear $x_2 \mapsto t x_1 + x_2$ and the scaling $x_2 \mapsto s x_2$ as
explicit affine polynomial equivalences, and proves the key formulas
\[
  a x_1^2 + b x_1x_2 + c x_2^2
  \;\longmapsto\;
  \Bigl(a - \frac{b^2}{4c}\Bigr) x_1^2 + c x_2^2
  \qquad (c \neq 0),
\]
and
\[
  a x_1^2 + b x_1x_2
  \;\longmapsto\;
  b x_1x_2
  \qquad (b \neq 0),
\]
together with the diagonal scaling formula. So the remaining work is no longer
to discover the completion-of-squares algebra, but to connect these explicit
affine moves to a general rank-$4$ mixed-affine factor and then feed the result
into the already-formalized representative theorems.

\section*{Current Candidate Blueprint}
At the level of a paper proof, the present candidate argument is:

\begin{enumerate}
\item If the four coordinates of $u$ are linearly dependent over $\mathbb{R}$,
apply the already-formalized constant-relation lemma.
\item Otherwise let $W = \operatorname{span}(u_1,u_2,u_3,u_4)$ and classify by
the dimension of $W \cap \mathbb{R}[x_1,x_2]_{\le 1}$.
\item If $\dim(W \cap \mathbb{R}[x_1,x_2]_{\le 1}) = 3$, choose coefficient
  vectors spanning $1,x_1,x_2$ inside $W$ and apply the abstract theorem
  \texttt{residual\_eq\_zero\_of\_contains\_aff1}. No exact representative
  normalization is needed in this branch.
\item Otherwise use orthogonal changes in the factor coordinates together with
  affine changes of variables in $(x_1,x_2)$ to reduce the remaining stable
  branches to the following models:
  \[
    (x_1,x_2,x_1^2,x_1x_2),\quad
    (x_1,x_2,x_1^2,x_2^2),\quad
    (1,x_1^2,x_1x_2,x_2^2),\quad
    (x_1,x_1^2,x_1x_2,x_2^2).
  \]
\item In these stable models, show directly that every quadratic square lies in
  \[
    \operatorname{im}(A_u) + \operatorname{cone}(\sigma(\ker A_u))
  \]
  using explicit kernel families:
  \begin{itemize}
  \item $(x_1,x_2,x_1^2,x_1x_2)$:
    \[
      w_c=(-2x_2,x_1,0,1),\qquad
      w_q=(-x_2^2,x_1x_2,0,0);
    \]
  \item $(x_1,x_2,x_1^2,x_2^2)$:
    \[
      w=(-x_1,0,1,0);
    \]
  \item $(1,x_1^2,x_1x_2,x_2^2)$: surjective image, no kernel term needed;
  \item $(x_1,x_1^2,x_1x_2,x_2^2)$:
    \[
      w_\ell=(-x_2\ell,0,\ell,0),\qquad \ell=c+d x_2.
    \]
  \end{itemize}
\item The model
  \[
    (1,x_1^2,x_1x_2,x_2^2)
  \]
  is already formalized: its image is all quartics.
\item Treat the mixed-affine branch
  \[
    (1,x_1,x_1^2,x_1x_2),\qquad
    (1,x_1,x_1x_2,x_2^2),\qquad
    (1,x_1,x_1^2,x_2^2)
  \]
  separately. The representative quotient generators are now explicit:
  the first model uses the family
  \[
    w_\ell=(0,0,-x_2\ell,x_1\ell),\qquad \ell=c+d x_2,
  \]
  the second uses
  \[
    w=(0,0,-x_1x_2,x_1^2),
  \]
  and the third is now formalized as surjective. What is still missing is the written
  proof that every constant-containing $\dim = 2$ factor can be normalized into
  one of these representatives.
\item A smaller replacement for exact representative transport is now plausible
  in this branch: prove basis-invariant mixed-affine plane theorems. Instead of
  forcing the last two coordinates to be exactly
  \[
    (x_1^2,x_1x_2)\quad\text{or}\quad(x_1x_2,x_2^2),
  \]
  one can work with an arbitrary orthonormal coefficient-space basis of the
  quadratic plane
  \[
    x_1 \cdot \operatorname{span}(x_1,x_2)
    \qquad\text{or}\qquad
    x_2 \cdot \operatorname{span}(x_1,x_2),
  \]
  and build the kernel family directly from the syzygy
  \[
    (x_1 a)(b \ell) - (x_1 b)(a \ell)=0
  \]
  or its $x_2$-analogue. In these plane-type formulations, the image part
  depends only on the plane itself, while orthonormality restores the clean
  $\sigma = (a^2+b^2)\ell^2$ identity used in the representative proofs. The
  current Lean support for this approach is now in place: the new file
  \texttt{RepresentativeMixedAffinePlane.lean} proves that any explicit scalar
  relation $\sum c_i u_i=r$ yields an admissible image witness for $rq$, that
  two orthonormal coefficient-space relation directions satisfy
  \[
    \sigma\bigl(\mathrm{rel}_c(p)+\mathrm{rel}_d(q)\bigr)=p^2+q^2.
  \]
  More importantly, it now abstracts the whole image half of the rank-$14$
  mixed-affine proof: if a factor admits explicit relations for
  \[
    1,\qquad x_1,\qquad x_1x_2,\qquad x_2^2,
  \]
  then every quartic with zero $x_1^4$ coefficient lies in its admissible
  image. The certificate half is now abstracted too: if those same relations
  are accompanied by an orthonormal coefficient-space pair for the
  $(x_1x_2,x_2^2)$ directions, then the formalized kernel
  \[
    w=\mathrm{rel}_{-c_2}(t x_1x_2)+\mathrm{rel}_{c_3}(t x_1^2)
  \]
  matches the missing $x_1^4$ coefficient and forces zero residual for every
  SOCP. So the missing step is no longer the monomial image or kernel algebra
  itself, but the extraction of the correct canonical relation data from an
  arbitrary factor.
\item The rank-$13$ mixed-affine branch is now abstracted in the same style.
  The new plane-level theorem only assumes explicit scalar relations for
  \[
    1,\qquad x_1^2,\qquad x_1x_2,
  \]
  plus an orthonormal coefficient-space pair for the last two directions. Under
  those hypotheses, every quartic with vanishing $x_2^3$ and $x_2^4$
  coefficients lies in the admissible image, and the formalized kernel
  \[
    w=\mathrm{rel}_{-c_2}(x_2\ell)+\mathrm{rel}_{c_3}(x_1\ell),
    \qquad \ell=b+c x_2,
  \]
  matches the missing $(x_2^3,x_2^4)$ classes and forces zero residual for
  every SOCP. So both nonsurjective mixed-affine representatives are now
  available as basis-invariant plane certificates rather than only as concrete
  coordinate models.
\item Apply the formalized admissible image-plus-cone certificate to conclude
$\sigma(u)=p$.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent
The main obstacle to a finished Lean proof is now precise: complete the
normalization/classification of the constant-containing $\dim = 2$ branch, then
assemble the final theorem from the already-formalized representative and
abstract branch theorems.

\medskip

That six-monomial normal form is now formalized in
\texttt{TernaryQuarticProof/QuadraticNormalForm.lean}. It introduces the six

\[

proof-serving finite-support lemma rather than a general coefficient API.

\medskip

\section*{Pending Step}
To finish the theorem, it remains to prove the multivariate admissible
image-plus-cone decomposition for every SOS quartic. This is currently the main
mathematical gap. The likely form is a finite-dimensional quadratic-space
certificate rather than a direct copy of the univariate gcd/peeling argument.
